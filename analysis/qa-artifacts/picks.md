# Top 5 Large-Cap Picks

The tape just told you something important: Micron ripped and chips staged what Cramer called a "revenge trade" for [last week's misguided selling](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/06/jim-cramer-chip-stocks-are-staging-a-revenge-trade-for-last-weeks-misguided-selling.html), while the Dow closed at a record on [revived AI optimism](https://www.marketwatch.com/bulletins/redirect/go?g=d52a3a85-1ef3-4aec-8929-60d3ecb36342&mod=mw_rss_bulletins). But Morgan Stanley is flagging a rotation away from chipmakers into hyperscalers as the AI trade matures — that's the tension I'm underwriting across this list.

**1. AVGO — Broadcom Inc.**
The consensus view is Broadcom is a chip stock; the mispricing is that it's really a hyperscaler-capex derivative with better margins than any hyperscaler itself. Operating margin of 40.8% and `<term id="EBITDA">EV/EBITDA</term>` of 49.4x look rich against a 27.0x `<term id="PE">forward P/E</term>` equivalent, but that's the price of 36.2% net margins and a business increasingly levered to custom AI silicon (XPUs) rather than merchant GPU cyclicality. Shares are up 3.7% on the day as the chip complex snaps back. The variant view: as Morgan Stanley flags a pivot to hyperscalers, Broadcom sits at the intersection — it monopolizes the custom-silicon supply chain those same hyperscalers depend on. Risk: `<term id="EV">EV</term>`-to-sales of 27x leaves zero room for a slowdown in custom ASIC orders from the two or three customers that dominate that book.

**2. META — Meta Platforms, Inc.**
Meta trades at 28.1x earnings with a 30.2% net margin and 30.2% `<term id="ROE">ROE</term>` — cheaper on a P/E basis than Broadcom or Tesla despite comparable AI-capex torque. `<term id="EV">EV</term>/EBITDA` of 17.1x is the cheapest multiple of any mega-cap AI compounder in this list. The stock is up 3.0% today riding the same AI-optimism wave. The overlooked angle: Meta is a net beneficiary, not a victim, of the "Big Tech pocketing the AI equity" debate raised in [this MarketWatch piece](https://www.marketwatch.com/story/your-share-of-the-ai-wealth-is-a-right-not-a-handout-here-is-how-we-claw-back-our-money-7454b0e0?mod=mw_rss_topstories) — regulatory noise around data ownership is a tail risk, not a base case. Risk: `<term id="FCF">FCF</term>`-to-`<term id="OCF">OCF</term>` of just 0.40 shows capex intensity (reality-lab and AI infra spend) is eating a huge chunk of operating cash flow; any capex guide-up compresses the free-cash-flow story further.

**3. TSLA — Tesla, Inc.**
This is the highest-risk, highest-torque name on the list and I'm ranking it here purely on momentum plus optionality, not on fundamentals — at 418x trailing earnings and 254.9x `<term id="FCF">free cash flow</term>`, valuation offers no margin of safety. But the stock is up 6.7% today, the single largest mover among mega-caps, and [MarketWatch flags Tesla/SpaceX-related volatility now exceeding the Nasdaq-100's own historical vol](https://www.marketwatch.com/story/the-nasdaq-100-has-been-far-more-volatile-than-the-s-p-500-now-add-spacex-to-the-mix-cd397c36?mod=mw_rss_topstories) — that's a signal this name is trading on catalyst-driven repricing, not fundamentals. Operating margin is a thin 5.1%, net margin 4.0% — the market is paying for optionality on robotaxi/AI, not the auto business. Risk: any miss on autonomy timelines or margin compression in the core EV business (already thin at 18.0% gross margin) triggers a violent multiple reset.

**4. ORCL — Oracle Corporation**
Oracle's book is a house-of-cards on the surface — `<term id="ROE">ROE</term>` of 53.4% only because the equity base is thin, and `<term id="FCF">free cash flow</term>` is negative (`<term id="FCF">FCF</term>`-to-`<term id="OCF">OCF</term>` ratio of -0.74) due to heavyweight AI-data-center capex of $19.10/share. Stock is up 2.5% today. The variant call: Oracle's `<term id="EBITDA">EV/EBITDA</term>` of 21.9x is a full turn cheaper than Broadcom's and in line with legacy-software peers, despite Oracle now running one of the largest AI-cloud infrastructure backlogs in the industry (OCI). The market is still pricing this as a database company, not a hyperscaler challenger. Risk: negative free cash flow means the AI infrastructure bet has to pay off on schedule — if cloud bookings decelerate, the balance sheet (interest debt per share of $85.8M scaled) gets uncomfortable fast.

**5. JPM — JP Morgan Chase & Co.**
The only non-tech name here, included because it's the cheapest quality compounder on the list: 16.2x earnings, 2.6x `<term id="PB">price-to-book</term>`, and a 9.0% dividend yield-adjusted return profile most AI names can't touch. Up 1.4% today in a rally where [stocks are also getting a seasonal tailwind as Congress recesses](https://www.marketwatch.com/story/stocks-rally-when-congress-goes-on-summer-break-here-is-the-hidden-reason-e70a6be3?mod=mw_rss_topstories). With bitcoin rebounding on [Trump's crypto comments](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/06/bitcoin-rebounds-after-trump-says-hes-become-a-big-crypto-guy.html) and Klarna [pushing into a U.S. bank charter](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/06/klarna-seeks-us-bank-charter-in-push-beyond-buy-now-pay-later.html), the financial-services landscape is shifting — JPM is the incumbent best positioned to absorb fintech disruption rather than be disrupted by it, given its scale in payments and capital markets. Risk: `<term id="ROA">ROA</term>` of just 1.3% reflects a heavily levered balance sheet; any credit-cycle turn hits net income disproportionately.

# Top 5 Small-Cap & Growth Picks

**1. LMND — Lemonade, Inc.**
The standout mover of the day, up 10.0% to $78.70 on a $6.05B market cap. Lemonade is an AI-underwriting insurtech finally scaling — this is the name Morgan Stanley's "hyperscaler pivot" thesis quietly supports, since Lemonade's moat is proprietary claims-AI, not GPU access. The risk/reward here is a momentum breakout on top of a business inflecting toward profitability; the catalyst is the stock's outsized reaction versus a broad tape that was only up 0.7-1.1% on the major indices. Risk: still a story stock — no profitability metrics in this list, so any deceleration in premium growth or loss-ratio miss reverses the move fast.

**2. ACHR — Archer Aviation Inc.**
Up 7.8% to $5.37 on a $4.1B market cap, riding the same eVTOL enthusiasm lifting Joby. This sits adjacent to the SpaceX/aerospace thematic already getting attention — [Yahoo Finance flagged SpaceX's next catalyst](https://finance.yahoo.com/technology/live/tech-stocks-live-spacex-stock-gets-its-next-catalyst-samsung-earnings-on-deck-131003284.html) driving renewed interest in the whole next-gen aerospace complex today. Archer is a pure-play regulatory-catalyst name: certification progress moves this stock double digits in a session. Risk: pre-revenue at scale, cash-burn dependent, and any FAA timeline slippage is a violent de-rating catalyst in the other direction.

**3. INDI — indie Semiconductor, Inc.**
Up 6.9% to $4.63, a sub-$1B microcap semiconductor name riding the same chip-sector "return to optimism" that lifted Micron today, per [MarketWatch](https://www.marketwatch.com/story/microns-stock-gains-signaling-a-return-to-optimism-about-the-chip-sector-0b7f6a8c?mod=mw_rss_topstories). Indie is a pure-play auto-semiconductor name (ADAS, sensor fusion) — smallest-cap, highest-beta way to play the same chip re-rating driving the mega-cap semi names in the portfolio higher, without paying Nvidia/Broadcom multiples. Risk: extremely thin float and market cap means any broad tech de-risking hits this name disproportionately hard; auto-semi demand is also cyclical and exposed to EV production cuts.

**4. SHLS — Shoals Technologies Group, Inc.**
Up 6.3% to $10.58, a $1.8B solar-electrical-BOS name. This is a torque play on the [renewables case getting strengthened by the Iran war](https://oilprice.com/Alternative-Energy/Renewable-Energy/Iran-War-Strengthens-the-Case-for-Renewables-and-Storage.html) — geopolitical risk premium on oil is pushing capital back toward solar/storage infrastructure names that got left for dead in the recent rate-driven selloff. Shoals makes the electrical balance-of-system components for utility-scale solar — high operating leverage to any reacceleration in utility-scale solar bookings. Risk: rate-sensitive capex cycle; if long rates back up again, project financing for utility solar slows and this reverses.

**5. FLNC — Fluence Energy, Inc.**
Up 4.8% to $17.82, a $3.3B energy-storage integrator — the direct read-through from the same [Iran war / renewables-and-storage thesis](https://oilprice.com/Alternative-Energy/Renewable-Energy/Iran-War-Strengthens-the-Case-for-Renewables-and-Storage.html) benefiting Shoals, but Fluence sits closer to the grid-storage side of the AI-power-demand story — data centers are driving grid-storage buildout as much as solar is. This is a smaller, higher-beta way to play the same electrical-infrastructure theme as Vertiv without paying Vertiv's 29.4x `<term id="EBITDA">EV/EBITDA</term>`. Risk: Fluence has a history of margin volatility on fixed-price contracts; any commodity input cost spike (battery cells, steel) compresses margins quickly.
